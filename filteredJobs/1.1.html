Chair of Machine Tools

Our Profile

The Laboratory for Machine Tools and Production Engineering (WZL) at RWTH Aachen University enhances the innovative strength and competitiveness of industry through groundbreaking basic research, applied research, and the resulting consulting and implementation projects in the field of industrial production. In collaboration with industrial partners from various sectors, it develops practical solutions for future-oriented value creation systems. Within the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) research group, the Department of Automation and Control Technology at the Chair of Machine Tools addresses complex issues at the interface between mechanical engineering and computer science.

The Industrial Internet of Things involves using smart sensors, actuators, and other devices, such as radio frequency transceivers, to improve manufacturing and industrial processes. In the IIoT, devices are typically networked to collect, share, and analyze data, with insights from these processes enhancing efficiency and reliability. Wireless communication is increasingly becoming an indispensable part of smart manufacturing and already plays a central role. The development and evaluation of advanced sensor technologies, hardware interfaces, and communication protocols for the effective interaction of various components within wireless sensor-actuator networks is a key focus of current research and a core aspect of the advertised position. Our environment offers numerous opportunities for personal development and provides potential career perspectives, including:

  • Collaboration in research operations within the topics of Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT), Industrial Cyber-Physical Systems (ICPS), and Wireless Sensor and Actuator Networks (WSAN).
  • Expansion and sharpening of your personal and professional skill set through rigorous scientific work.
  • The opportunity to contribute to internationally recognized publications in prestigious journals or conferences.
  • Flexible working hours (e.g., consideration of exam dates).
  • The possibility of writing a thesis, if interested.

Your Profile

  • You are prepared to demonstrate above-average commitment to demanding tasks.
  • You have an interest in programming tasks and the ability to familiarize yourself with new, complex technical issues.
  • You are willing to responsibly carry out assigned tasks.
  • You already have programming skills in C, C++, Python, or other programming languages.
  • You are currently studying mathematics, physics, or engineering sciences.
  • You have excellent English skills for conducting research in international publications.

Interested? We are happy to hear that! Please send your application documents (short letter of motivation, CV, and current grades; optionally, include a link to your GitHub profile or other references regarding programming experience from private, university, and professional projects) preperably via email in a PDF file to the specified contact person.

Your Duties and Responsibilities

  • Support in programming microcontrollers and developing applications for analyzing sensor data in the C programming language.
  • Support in implementing mathematical methods for iterative estimation of parameters to describe system states (e.g., Kalman filter) and solving nonlinear optimization problems in the C programming language.
  • Participation in scientifically based literature research: systematic review, meta-analysis, meta-ethnography, and meta-synthesis.
  • Preparation, execution, and follow-up of measurement investigations.
  • Taking on various cross-sectional tasks in ongoing research and industrial projects.

What We Offer

The successful candidate will be employed as a student assistant.
The position is to be filled at the earliest possible date and offered for a fixed term of 12 months.
This is a part-time contract position.
The standard weekly hours will be 12 hours.
The salary is based on the RWTH Guidelines for Student and Graduate Assistants.
The position corresponds to a pay grade of 13.25 euros per hour.

About us

RWTH is a certified family-friendly University. We support our employees in maintaining a good work-life balance with a wide range of health, advising, and prevention services, for example university sports. Employees who are covered by collective bargaining agreements and civil servants have access to an extensive range of further training courses and the opportunity to purchase a job ticket.
RWTH is an equal opportunities employer. We therefore welcome and encourage applications from all suitably qualified candidates, particularly from groups that are underrepresented at the University. All qualified applicants will receive consideration for employment and will not be discriminated against on the basis of national or ethnic origin, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, religion, disability or age. RWTH is strongly committed to encouraging women in their careers. Female applicants are given preference if they are equally suitable, competent, and professionally qualified, unless a fellow candidate is favored for a specific reason.
As RWTH is committed to equality of opportunity, we ask you not to include a photo in your application.
You can find information on the personal data we collect from applicants in accordance with Articles 13 and 14 of the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) at http://www.rwth-aachen.de/dsgvo-information-bewerbung.
